The secret of Tweet lengths

“How long is a Tweet?”

This interactive visualization shows how Tweet lengths have changed since the platform launched in 2006. The lines represent Tweet lengths over time and if you place cursor over them, you can see their relative positions compared to other lengths at that time. If you click on them, you can see the percentage changes too.

So, if you place your cursor on 139 characters, it shows that 139 is ranked 60th in June 2013 — compared to 140 characters, which is first. Clicking on 139 characters shows that it is actually the length of less than 1% of all Tweets.

Click "random" or the "-" and "+" buttons to explore the data in more ways. You can also use the arrow keys (← , →) instead of "-" and "+" buttons.
